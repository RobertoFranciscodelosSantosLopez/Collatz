
Bit-Dynamics and Parity Decisiveness: Empirical Validation
This repository contains the computational evidence and formal scripts supporting the research paper submitted to the Journal of Number Theory. Our research introduces a new framework for the 3n+1 conjecture based on Intrinsic Bit-Ratio (E) and the Law of Suicidal Expansion.

1. Core Discovery: The Intrinsic Ratio (E)
We postulate that the Collatz process is a dissipative system where entropy (bit-length) is governed by the ratio between bit extraction (kr) and bit injection (ke).

Theoretical Threshold: The growth limit for a 3n+1 system is log2(3) approx. 1.585.

Axiomatic Prediction: E[kr/ke] = 2.0.

The Law of Suicidal Expansion: As n approaches infinity, the probability of encountering high-order reductive sinks increases, enforcing a deterministic decay toward the {4, 2, 1} attractor.

2. Experimental Results at Scale
While traditional verification methods have reached 2^68 through linear search, this research validates the Bit-Dynamics model using high-entropy seeds up to 1,000,000 bits.
